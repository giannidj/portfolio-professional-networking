Hack The Future – Deep Dive into Linux Waters

Recently, I participated in Hack The Future in Antwerp, a full-day hackathon organised by De Cronos Groep. The event brought together students and companies for a day focused on coding, collaboration, troubleshooting and problem solving through a variety of technical challenges.

Together with a teammate, I worked on Challenge 112: *Deep Dive into Linux Waters*, organised by Piros / QUBR.

The challenge

The challenge focused on Linux, automation and containerization. Each team received access to a real Linux server and had to explore and configure the environment using various open-source technologies.

The challenge description emphasized experimentation, teamwork and technical problem solving in a Linux environment. The main technologies involved were:

- Linux

- Ansible

- Containers

- Podman

- Docker concepts

- Nginx

Participants were expected to work directly through the command line and use automation tools to manage and configure services on the server.

What we worked on

During the day, we explored the Linux server environment and worked with Podman containers and Ansible automation. The challenge required a combination of troubleshooting, experimentation and collaboration to make progress.

Although I already had some prior experience with Linux and containers, the hackathon gave me the opportunity to refresh existing knowledge while also learning new concepts and workflows throughout the day.

One aspect I particularly enjoyed was working on a real server instead of a purely theoretical setup. This made the challenge feel much more practical and realistic.

Reflection

I found this event very valuable because it combined multiple important aspects of IT in one experience:

- technical problem solving,

- teamwork,

- infrastructure management,

- and learning under time pressure.

Hackathons create a very different environment compared to traditional classes. Instead of following step-by-step exercises, we had to figure things out ourselves, search documentation, troubleshoot issues and make decisions as a team.

I especially appreciated the focus on Linux and automation because these technologies are widely used in modern infrastructure and DevOps environments. Working with tools such as Ansible and Podman also showed how important automation and containerization have become in current IT environments.

Another positive aspect was the interaction with companies and professionals present at the event. It was interesting to see how businesses organize technical challenges around technologies that are actively used in industry.

What I learned

This challenge helped me improve:

- Linux command line skills,

- troubleshooting abilities,

- understanding of containers,

- basic automation workflows with Ansible,

- and collaboration under time pressure.

It also reinforced the importance of documentation and experimentation when working with unfamiliar systems.

Would I participate again?

Definitely. Hack The Future was both educational and enjoyable. The combination of competition, teamwork and practical technology created a motivating atmosphere.

I would absolutely participate again in future hackathons because they are a great way to improve technical skills, gain practical experience and discover technologies outside of regular coursework.
