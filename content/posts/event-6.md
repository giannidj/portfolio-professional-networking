# Tech \& Meet – The Defense Cybersecurity Landscape in Belgium



Later in December, I attended another Tech \& Meet event focused on the cybersecurity landscape in Belgium. While the previous session explored cybersecurity from an international NATO perspective, this event focused much more on how Belgium itself organizes cyber defence and national cybersecurity efforts.



The session featured speakers from organisations such as the Centre for Cybersecurity Belgium (CCB), the Belgian Cyber Force / Cyber Command and EgovSelect.



## Content of the session



The event aimed to provide an overview of the most important cybersecurity organisations operating in Belgium and explain how they contribute to protecting the country against cyber threats.



Several organisations and acronyms were discussed, including:

\- CCB,

\- CERT,

\- Cyber Command,

\- FCCU,

\- NCCN,

\- VSSE,

\- and SGRS.



One of the goals of the session was to help participants “see the forest for the trees” and better understand the role of each organisation within Belgium’s cybersecurity ecosystem.



The speakers also explained:

\- what skills are needed in cybersecurity roles,

\- how recruitment works for defense and government positions,

\- and what career paths exist within these organisations.



An important message throughout the event was that cybersecurity is not limited to “hackers” or purely technical experts. Belgium also needs:

\- analysts,

\- instructors,

\- policy specialists,

\- network experts,

\- cloud specialists,

\- researchers,

\- digital forensics experts,

\- and threat hunters.



## Reflection



I found this event particularly valuable because it showed how broad the cybersecurity field actually is.



The session demonstrated that defending a country digitally requires collaboration between many different organisations and disciplines. Cybersecurity is not only about technology but also about communication, intelligence, operations, infrastructure and policy.



One interesting point discussed during the presentation was how modern military systems generate enormous amounts of data. For example, unmanned drones can produce multiple terabytes of data per minute. This illustrates how strongly cybersecurity and data processing are becoming integrated into defense operations.



Another point that stood out to me was that modern soldiers themselves are increasingly connected through digital systems, making them potential targets for cyberattacks as well.



I also appreciated the realistic explanation of defensive and offensive cyber capabilities. The speakers explained how cybersecurity within defense environments often requires understanding both sides in order to effectively protect systems and infrastructure.



## Personal takeaway



This session gave me a much clearer understanding of the Belgian cybersecurity landscape and the many opportunities available within the field.



It was interesting to see how organisations such as the CCB and Cyber Command are actively expanding and investing in cybersecurity capabilities as cyber threats continue to increase, especially with the growing influence of AI-assisted attacks.



The event also reinforced the idea that cybersecurity will only become more important in the future, both nationally and internationally.



## Would I recommend it?



Yes. I would definitely recommend this session to students interested in cybersecurity, networking, digital forensics or defense-related IT.



The presentations provided a very accessible overview of Belgium’s cybersecurity ecosystem while also showing the real-world importance and impact of cyber defence today.
