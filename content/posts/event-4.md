# Tech \& Meet – The Transition to IPv6



Last week, I attended a Tech \& Meet evening session about IPv6 presented by Nico Declerck. The session focused on the ongoing transition from IPv4 to IPv6, the importance of dual-stack environments and the security considerations involved in modern network infrastructures.



The presentation was aimed at both networking professionals and students interested in understanding why IPv6 adoption is becoming increasingly important today.



## About the speaker



Nico Declerck is an experienced network administrator and Linux system administrator. Besides working in networking and cybersecurity, he is also a lecturer in the Bachelor Applied Informatics and Bachelor CyberSecurity programmes at Howest University of Applied Sciences.



In addition, he is active as:

\- researcher within Cluster IT,

\- Cisco Networking Instructor,

\- Cisco Instructor Trainer,

\- and advisor for BiASC (Belgian IT Academy Support Center).



His practical experience in networking and cybersecurity clearly reflected throughout the presentation.



## Content of the session



The event was divided into three major parts:



### Why IPv6?



The session started with an explanation of the origins and limitations of IPv4. Topics such as address exhaustion and the rise of NAT were discussed to explain why IPv6 became necessary.



The speaker emphasized that the transition to IPv6 can no longer be postponed, especially as more ISPs, enterprises and now even SMEs are adopting IPv6 and dual-stack environments.



### Transitioning to IPv6-only



The second part focused on future scenarios where IPv4 support may gradually disappear. Different transition strategies, risks and challenges were discussed, including compatibility and infrastructure concerns.



I found this section especially interesting because it highlighted that IPv6 is no longer simply “future technology”, but something organisations are actively preparing for today.



### Dual Stack in Practice



The final part of the presentation focused on practical deployment strategies for dual-stack environments.



Topics included:

\- routing,

\- firewalls,

\- IPv6 configurations,

\- and security-by-design principles.



The practical examples helped make the technical concepts easier to understand and demonstrated how organisations can start implementing IPv6 incrementally instead of performing a full migration at once.



## Reflection



I found this session very insightful because IPv6 is a topic that is often mentioned in networking courses, but not always explored from a practical deployment perspective.



One of the strongest messages from the presentation was that the transition is already happening. IPv6 adoption is no longer limited to large enterprises or ISPs; smaller organisations are increasingly being confronted with the need to support IPv6 as well.



The combination of technical explanations and practical examples made the session both educational and accessible. I also appreciated how security considerations were integrated throughout the presentation instead of being treated as a separate topic.



Another enjoyable aspect was the presentation style itself. Nico Declerck occasionally included anecdotes about Bruges, its history and local beers, which made the session more engaging and informal.



## What I learned



This session helped me better understand:

\- why IPv4 limitations led to IPv6 adoption,

\- the role of NAT in modern networks,

\- how dual-stack environments operate,

\- and the security implications of IPv6 deployments.



It also reinforced the importance of staying up to date with evolving networking technologies, especially in cybersecurity and infrastructure-related fields.
